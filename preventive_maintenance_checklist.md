# LinkedIn Post

Data center operations and production operations are more similar than people think.

Both require uptime, reliability, capacity planning, preventive maintenance, incident response, and audit-ready documentation.

That is why I built a Data Center Operations Framework based on five pillars:

1. Infrastructure Reliability
2. Capacity & Resource Planning
3. Incident Management
4. Compliance & Risk Control
5. Workflow Optimization & Automation

My approach is simple:

Operate the data center like a production facility — measurable, repeatable, monitored, and continuously improved.

The goal is not only to respond to problems faster.

The goal is to build systems that reduce problems before they happen.

Key metrics I would track include uptime, MTTR, MTBF, rack utilization, power usage efficiency, SLA compliance, and repeat incident rate.

This framework connects my background in operations, production planning, inventory control, and compliance with the reliability mindset required in data center environments.

Strong operations are not accidental.

They are designed.
